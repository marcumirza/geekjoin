The Problem

There's a gap between
learning and employment.

Students graduate with theory but no real project experience. Companies struggle to judge candidates from interviews alone. Something needs to bridge that gap.

🎓

Students lack real experience

Academic projects don't reflect real-world teamwork, deadlines, documentation, or product thinking. Graduates struggle to prove what they can actually do.

📄

Resumes don't tell the whole story

A polished CV can't capture how someone collaborates, solves problems under pressure, or grows over time. Hiring based on resumes is a gamble.

🔍

Talent is hard to discover

Exceptional beginners and motivated students go unnoticed because they don't have the right network or portfolio to reach companies on their own.

How It Works

From idea to opportunity
in four steps.

Whether you're a builder with an idea or a contributor looking to grow, TeamUp makes the path forward clear.

1

Create or join a project

Post your app idea and define the roles you need — or browse active projects and request to join based on your skills. You can focus on what you love while collaborating on the parts you want to learn.

2

Build with structure

Set up milestones, assign tasks, and work through the full development process — planning, implementation, testing, documentation, security reviews, and more. Real teamwork, real practices.

3

Document your progress publicly

Every step of your project is logged on a transparent timeline. Your contributions, decisions, and growth become a living portfolio that tells your story far better than any resume.

4

Get discovered by companies

Companies browse and observe active projects on TeamUp. When they spot talent they want — whether that's you or your whole team — they reach out directly. Your work does the talking.
