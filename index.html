The Problem

There's a gap between
university and tech jobs.

Tech and engineering students graduate with theory but no real shipping experience. Tech companies struggle to evaluate junior developers from interviews and CVs alone.

🎓

Students lack shipping experience

University coursework rarely reflects how real software teams work — sprints, code review, deployments, debugging in production. Graduates struggle to prove they can actually build and ship.

📄

Resumes & GitHub don't tell the full story

A CV lists tech stacks. A GitHub shows commits. Neither shows how a developer collaborates, debugs under pressure, or contributes to a real product team.

🔍

Junior dev talent stays invisible

Talented junior developers and designers go unnoticed because they don't have the network, portfolio, or shipped products to reach tech recruiters yet.

Why?

Built by a student.
For students who want more.

I started GeekJoin because I lived the gap.

I studied Robotics Engineering and taught myself programming on the side. But theory and solo tutorials only got me so far — I wanted to build something real with my own hands, with a clear purpose, alongside people who could push me forward. Working alone, without direction, wasn't teaching me what I actually needed to become a good engineer.

When I looked at my CV, I saw coursework no recruiter cared about. I couldn't talk confidently in interviews because I had no shipped products, no team workflows, no real story to tell. I wanted a platform where I could build real things with real teams, learn how engineering actually works, and let companies see how I think — not just what my CV claimed.

The second reason is connection. Students all over the world are studying the same subjects, but learning them in completely different ways — different perspectives, different mindsets, different solutions to the same problem. A platform that brings motivated builders together across countries and backgrounds creates the kind of shared learning a single university simply can't. And it gives companies a window into talent they'd never otherwise reach.

GeekJoin is the platform I needed when I started. I'm building it so the next generation of developers doesn't have to start from zero.
